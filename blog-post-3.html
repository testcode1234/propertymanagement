Navigating San Diego's Rental Laws: What Owners Need to Know

San Diego's rental laws, shaped by California's strict regulations, can be complex for property owners. Understanding these rules ensures compliance and protects your investment. Here's a guide to key legal considerations.

1. Rent Control Regulations

California's Tenant Protection Act (AB 1482) caps annual rent increases at 5% plus inflation (up to 10%) for certain properties. Exemptions apply to newer buildings or single-family homes not owned by corporations. We can help determine if your property is exempt.

2. Just Cause Evictions

Landlords must provide a valid reason for evictions, such as non-payment or lease violations. "No-fault" evictions (e.g., owner move-in) require relocation assistance. Our team ensures eviction processes comply with state law.

3. Security Deposit Rules

Security deposits are limited to two months' rent for unfurnished units or three months for furnished. Refunds must be returned within 21 days with an itemized deduction list. We handle deposits to ensure compliance.

4. Habitability Requirements

Properties must meet basic standards, including working plumbing, heating, and safe electrical systems. Regular maintenance prevents habitability disputes. Our maintenance services keep your property compliant.

5. Local Ordinances

San Diego has specific rules, such as tenant notification requirements for inspections. Staying updated is critical to avoid fines. Options Property Management monitors local laws to keep you compliant.

Navigating these laws can be daunting, but our expertise ensures you stay compliant while maximizing your investment. Contact us for tailored legal guidance.
