Even in sunny San Diego, winter brings unique maintenance challenges. While we don't face snow and freezing pipes, our rainy season requires specific preparation to protect your rental property and avoid costly emergency repairs.

Why Winter Maintenance Matters in San Diego

San Diego's winter typically brings 10-12 inches of rainfall concentrated between November and March. After dry summer months, this sudden moisture can expose weaknesses in roofs, drainage systems, and weatherproofing that have gone unnoticed.

Essential Winter Maintenance Tasks

1. Roof Inspection and Gutters

Your roof is your property's first defense against rain:

  • Inspect for missing, cracked, or curling shingles
  • Check flashing around chimneys and vents
  • Clean gutters and downspouts thoroughly
  • Ensure downspouts direct water away from foundation
  • Look for signs of leaks in attic or ceiling

2. HVAC System Service

Heating is essential during San Diego's cool nights:

  • Replace furnace filters
  • Test heating system before cold weather arrives
  • Check thermostat operation
  • Schedule professional HVAC inspection
  • Clean vents and registers

3. Exterior Drainage

Proper drainage prevents water intrusion:

  • Ensure property slopes away from foundation
  • Clear debris from drainage channels
  • Check for standing water areas
  • Inspect and clean French drains if present
  • Test sump pumps where applicable

4. Windows and Doors

Seal gaps to prevent water intrusion and heat loss:

  • Inspect and replace worn weatherstripping
  • Check window and door seals
  • Caulk gaps around frames
  • Test windows and doors for proper closure
  • Check for water stains indicating leaks

5. Plumbing Preparation

While San Diego rarely freezes, protection is smart:

  • Insulate exposed pipes in garages or crawl spaces
  • Disconnect and drain outdoor hoses
  • Cover outdoor faucets
  • Check for slow drips indicating clogs
  • Inspect water heater for signs of wear

6. Landscape and Trees

Prevent storm damage from trees and debris:

  • Trim branches hanging over roof
  • Remove dead or dying trees
  • Clear leaves and debris from property
  • Check irrigation systems for leaks
  • Adjust watering schedules for rain

üí° Preventive Maintenance Pays Off

A $300 roof inspection and gutter cleaning can prevent a $5,000+ water damage repair. Proactive winter maintenance protects your property value and keeps tenants comfortable during the rainy season.

Emergency Preparation

Prepare for winter storms:

  • Provide tenants with emergency contact numbers
  • Ensure tenants know water shut-off location
  • Keep list of emergency contractors ready
  • Document property condition with photos
  • Review insurance coverage for winter damage
